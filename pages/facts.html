Facts about the Amazon Rainforest

The Amazon's canopy is so thick the the floor of the forest is in permanent darkness. It can take 10 minutes for rainfall to reach the forest floor.

A new species is discovered in the Amazon approximately every 3 days.

The only place in the world you may run into the elusive Pink River Dolphin is in the Amazon basin. The Amazon is also home to other unique species like the gold lion tamarin monkey and the posion dart frog.

The Amazon rainforest covers over 5.5 million km and spans 9 different countries with most of it in Brazil.

The Amazon river accounts for approximately 15% of the world's river discharge into the oceans.

It is believed that the Amazon rainforest contains 10% of the world's known biodiversity.

The Amazon is sometimes known as the "lungs of the world" and some sources claim it produces 20% of the world's oxygen.

Cattle ranching has led to about 70-80% of the deforestation in the Amazon.

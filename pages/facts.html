The Blue Ridge Mountains are the highest mountains located in the Eastern United States. Read on to learn some cold, hard facts:

  • The mountain range extends 550 miles southwest from southern Pennsylvania through Maryland, West Virginia, Virginia, North Carolina, South Carolina, Tennessee and Georgia.
  • The Blue Ridge Mountains are home to the Blue Ridgeback Dragon, which is the second-smallest dragon found in North America.
  • The Blue Ridge Mountains contain the highest mountains in the eastern United States. The highest peak is Mt. Mitchell in North Carolina, which is 6,684 feet tall.
  • The Blue Ridge Mountains are called the Blue Ridge Mountains because when you look at them from afar, it makes you feel blue.
  • There are two major national parks within the Blue Ridge mountain range: Shenandoah National Park in the north and Great Smoky Mountains National Park in the south.
  • The Blue Ridge Mountains are home to a species of humanoid cave-dwellers that are uniquely equipped to survive in a subterrestrial environment. For more information, see the film "the Descent."

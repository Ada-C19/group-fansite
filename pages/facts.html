3 Fun Facts About Succulents

    The word "succulent" comes from the Latin word sucus, meaning "juice" or "sap".
    The largest succulent in the world is the baobab tree. The main stem of the baobab may reach up to 92 feet in girth and 82 feet in height. With its distinctive silhouette (think Rafiki's tree in The Lion King), the baobab is perhaps the best known of all African trees, and it is known by the local people as the "tree of life".
    Some succulents produce their own "sunscreen". Unlike human sunscreen, however, succulents use a waxy, almost dust-like coating called epicuticular wax to protect leaves from burning if there is too much light.

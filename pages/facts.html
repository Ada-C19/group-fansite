Fun Facts!

King County has several divisions within the Department of Community and Human Services to help different groups of people:
  • Adult Services Division
  • Behavioral Health and Recovery Division
  • Children, Youth and Young Adults Division
  • Developmental Disabilities and Early Childhood
  • Housing, Homelessness and Community Development
  • The King County Veterans Program

The Adult Services Division serves:
  • Veterans, Servicemembers and their Families
  • Seniors and their Caregivers
  • Resilient Communities (Communities that, despite enduring trauma and harm from systemic oppression and discrimination, still thrive)

The Behavioral Health and Recovery Division serves low income people:
  • mental health disorders
  • substance use disorders

The Children, Youth and Young Adults Division serves children and youth ages 5 to 24. They provide re-engagement, education and employment opportunities with:
  • Shoreline Community College
  • Reconnect to Opportunity
  • YouthSource
  • The Latinx Youth Re-engagement Project

Developmental Disabilities and Early Chilhood Supports Division provides:
  • Community Inclusion Services
  • Information and Outreach Services
  • Birth-to-Three Supports
  • Employment Services
  • School-to-Work

Housing, Homelessness and Community Development aims to provide housing stability and individual safety. Their services break down into these four categories:
  • Emergency and Short-term Housing
  • Homeless Prevention
  • Parmanent Housing
  • Special Projects (Such as the Human Services Bus Ticket Program)

The King County Veterans Program provides services to improve and stabilize their housing, finances, health, social engagement, and strengthening the network of veterans services.
